While You’re Away

25 October 2025, 10:46

The days move slow, love,
like clouds with no wind to guide them.
Your absence hums through my veins —
a quiet ache, a tender hush.

The room is still,
yet it hums with your memory.
Your laughter clings softly
to the corners of my heart.

The nights stretch endlessly —
the clock ticks slow, unkind.
I trace the places you once stood,
as if my gaze could call you back.

I miss you.
The days feel hollow without your voice —
hours pass, but they don’t live.
I reach for my phone,
half expecting your name to light the screen.

But only silence answers,
cold as rain on glass.
Even my laughter hides itself
when you’re away.

I keep your memory close,
like a candle that refuses to go out.
Still, sometimes I wonder —
do you feel this ache too?

It’s only a week, I tell myself,
but my heart doesn’t count time that way.
It counts by heartbeats,
and every one whispers your name.

— Caramine
